# Hetairoi Labs Pricing Strategy (Website)

## TL;DR
Use **hybrid transparency** on the website:
- Show clear **engagement models + starting prices**
- Do **not** show one fixed price card for all custom work
- Keep enterprise pricing as **"Custom"** with outcome-based scoping

This protects premium positioning while staying approachable for smaller buyers.

## Why this works (industry signals)
- Buyers increasingly expect pricing context before talking to sales. TrustRadius 2025 reports **49%** of buyers want more transparent pricing info.
- Gartner research shows strong preference for digital self-serve buying signals, so zero pricing context creates friction.
- Clutch 2026 pricing guide shows wide market spread by region and complexity, which supports showing ranges and floors instead of one universal quote.
- Many agencies that publish pricing publicly use **"Starting at"** and retainers, not rigid all-inclusive fixed quotes.

## Recommendation for Hetairoi

### 1) Pricing model to use
Adopt 3 lanes:

1. **Starter Sprint (fixed scope, low risk entry)**
- Best for small teams and first-time clients
- Example deliverables: discovery, architecture review, roadmap, technical audit
- Website display: **Starting at $2,500-$7,500**

2. **Build Engagement (project-based)**
- Best for MVPs and product delivery
- Outcome + scope based pricing with defined milestones
- Website display: **Starting at $15,000+**

3. **Embedded Partner (monthly retainer)**
- Best for growth-stage and enterprise
- Ongoing product engineering, AI systems, blockchain infra
- Website display: **From $6,000-$25,000+/month**

### 2) What not to show
- Avoid hourly rates as the primary public price anchor
- Avoid a giant feature matrix that makes services look commoditized
- Avoid "Contact us for pricing" with no range at all

### 3) How to avoid scaring small clients
- Keep a clear low-friction entry offer (Starter Sprint)
- Add one line under pricing: "Not sure yet? Book a free 30-minute fit call"
- Show what is included in the starter scope so price feels predictable

### 4) How to preserve premium value
- Use outcome language, not effort language
- Keep enterprise lane as **Custom** after discovery
- Add proof near pricing: case studies, results, reliability claims

## Website format that should convert well

## Section title
"Engagement Models" (better than "Pricing" if work is customized)

## Card format (recommended)
For each card:
- Who it is for
- What outcome they get
- Typical timeline
- Starting price
- CTA: "Book a fit call"

## Suggested website copy structure
1. Headline: "Flexible engagement, transparent starting points"
2. Subline: "Choose the model that fits your stage. Final scope is tailored after discovery."
3. 3 pricing cards (Starter / Build / Embedded)
4. Small FAQ under cards:
- Do you do fixed bids?
- Is there a minimum engagement?
- Can we start small and expand?

## Commercial guardrails
- Publish a **minimum engagement floor** to filter bad-fit leads
- Quote final price only after a scoped discovery
- Keep proposal options in **Good / Better / Best** packages in sales docs
- Review pricing quarterly using win-rate, deal cycle, and margin data

## Decision
Yes, display pricing context on the website.

Best format for Hetairoi: **starting ranges + engagement models + clear scope boundaries**, not fixed one-size-fits-all prices.
